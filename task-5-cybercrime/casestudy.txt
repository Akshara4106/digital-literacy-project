Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is one of the most common types of cybercrime where attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data. This is usually done through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be legitimate.

In a typical phishing attack, the attacker sends an email pretending to be from a trusted organization like a bank or online service. The message often creates a sense of urgency, asking the user to click a link and verify their details. Once the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website where their information is collected.

Students and individuals who are not aware of such threats are the most common targets. The consequences include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

This case study highlights the importance of being cautious while interacting with unknown emails and links online.
